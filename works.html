MakeShift

Play as Kit and explore the vast world of outer space. Fight through difficult challenges to take back what's yours!

In a future ruled by Cybersoldiers and government-backed cybernetics, few uncorrupted robots remain. Kit, one of the last, is trapped in a scrapyard guarded by robotic enforcers and sealed by a powerful magnet. After a daring flight through the yard, Kit disables the magnet—only to awaken a massive, car-crushing boss. Narrowly escaping, Kit flees to a neighboring city. There, he battles robot police, navigates neon-lit skyscrapers, and breaks into a stronghold to steal vital blueprints. Just as he succeeds, a helicopter drops a Super Cybersoldier. Dodging laser fire and returning blows, Kit defeats the enemy and escapes with the intel. The final mission leads to Makeshift Tech’s main plant. Fighting through bots, turrets, and experimental soldiers, Kit faces a towering tank. Using surrounding turrets to destroy it piece by piece, he downloads classified data from the supercomputer and escapes. With this intel, Kit gives humanity and robokind their first real chance at resistance.

Black Friday Blitz

Genre: Action-Adventure | Beat-‘em-Up | Satirical RPG

A 2D beat ‘em up game where you play as a shopper on Black Friday, brawling throughout the chaotic city.

In this chaotic holiday-themed action game, players control Jake Goodeal—a determined shopper in a family race to claim the legendary Key to the City, a prize that grants tax immunity and five years of daily discounts. The catch? Only one family can win it. As Black Friday descends, Jake must battle through waves of crazed shoppers, overworked store employees, and even mall Santas hired by the Mayor to keep competitors out. From the front gates of KO SALES to a showdown with the Secret Shopper Santa, and finally a desperate race against time as the Early Bird Sale Frenzy begins, Jake punches, dodges, and shops his way through a satirical retail warzone—all to bring glory to the Goodeals.

Shattered Realms

Everwild: The Forgotten Soul

Genre: Open world | Crafting | Satirical RPG

In her original world, 35-year-old Alex Lovett lived a quiet, unremarkable life—until a tragic accident ended it far too soon. Her death wasn’t meant to happen. A clerical mistake in the divine system cut her thread early, and the gods of a distant world—feeling an unusual pang of guilt—offered her a second chance. But instead of thrusting her into greatness or destiny, they granted her a simple wish: peace. Alex awakens in a vast, beautiful fantasy realm teeming with monsters, ancient magic, and bustling adventurers' towns. Choosing solitude, she builds a humble home deep in the Greenwood Expanse, a mystical forest where rare creatures roam and untapped mana pulses through the soil. Using her modern knowledge, sharp intuition, and newfound affinity for crafting and nature magic, Alex begins a quiet life. She gathers herbs, monster parts, and rare resources to craft potions, tools, and enchanted items to sell in nearby villages. Every sale buys her more time to train her spellcasting and study the world’s lore. Unbeknownst to her, however, the gods, flustered from their mistake, forgot to place a level cap on her soul. While others are bound to natural limits, Alex’s potential is infinite. The more she survives, learns, and fights, the faster her powers grow—slowly but surely eclipsing even the world’s strongest warriors. As players explore, they shape her path: Will she remain a recluse? Join the adventurers’ guild? Build a village? Or rise to challenge the god-kings themselves? The choice is theirs… and the world is watching.

Debug Island

3D Survival Adventure

Alex, a 35-year-old software engineer with zero real-world survival skills, crashes onto a remote island while en route to a tech conference. With no signal, no tools, and no stack overflow to rely on, Alex must now debug not code—but nature itself. By day, she learns to build shelter from driftwood and craft tools from scraps of the wreckage. By night, she battles the unknown, driven by hunger, fear, and the need to adapt. Every step forward is a patch on the biggest program she's ever written: her own survival. As players guide Alex through the island, they unlock survival skills like hunting, fire-making, and crafting—all while uncovering strange clues that suggest she might not be alone. It's not just about staying alive. It's about evolving.

The Maze

The Maze is a VR Multiplayer game in which human players arrive in a new realm after being teleported from their homelands. The size and purpose of the maze is unknown, the only thing that is certain is that they have to survive in this extremely hard environment. Upon arrival, players can choose any combination of two classes to fit their own play style. An example is merging both the Summoner and Warrior classes so that the player can act as a frontline fighter while at the same time summoning creatures to deal damage. Gunslinger and Druid are other examples; the player can deal damage from afar, root enemies into place and heal other teammates.

The Maze has dozens of different species to fight against. A few examples being Orcs, Trolls, Demons, Goblins, Elves, Undead, and Lizards. When Humans first arrived, the other species viewed them as nothing more than food or potential slaves. Humans aren’t as strong as Orcs, they don’t have the fast health regeneration of Trolls, nor do they have the high dexterity of Elves. Underestimating us will be their mortal mistake. They don't know what a mother and a father will go through to protect their loved ones, they don't know what a Human will endure to win their revenge, they don't know that there will be people that will sacrifice their lives for the freedom of others, they don’t know how people can do almost the impossible through faith, they don't know the creativity that Humans have, how we can create something beautiful as art, music, sculptures from just our imagination... However, we can also create atomic bombs and weapons of war. And they will know that there are Humans such as Napoleon, Sun Tzu, Alexander the Great, Julius Caesar see war as an art itself.

NeverDawn VR

The story starts at the end of an era and the beginning of a new one. Two souls from another dimension, similar to ours, decided to unite, to create a new world, letting them flee their reality. This world saw the birth of a new humanity, young and flourishing. Decades later, two trees saw the day at the creator’s effigy. Until the day when one of them began to corrupt. The hatred and pain of one creator grew. The corruption was materialized into energy, a demonic being. Spreading and consuming all living things on its way. Only the Lover’s Tree had resisted to it. The Founding City fell first and has never seen the dawn since that day. This was the beginning of the Neverdawn Era.
